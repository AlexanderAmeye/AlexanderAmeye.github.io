Introduction

Back in the late summer of 2019, I started looking into replicating a hand-drawn style within Unity. Specifically I wanted to successfully recreate the Ligne Claire style. The scene below was the first result in that attempt. It is a recreation of the panel from the right. This panel comes out The Black Island from The Adventures of Tintin created by Herg√©.



While I am happy with how this scene turned out in the end, the workflow of creating it was slow and convoluted while having several artistical restrictions that required workarounds. Specifically the method of drawing outlines provided me with little to no artistic control over where outlines were drawn. The outline shader is good for drawing outlines on bigger geometry like the tunnel on the right, but when it comes to little details like the brick wall or the train, the shader was really hard to fine-tune.

Improved Rendering

At the end of March 2020, I revisited my rendering system and tried new approaches that would allow me to implement an improved workflow for creating scenes in a hand-drawn style. My new system had to provide me control over the following 2 important aspects of the linework.

  1. Visuals (adjustable in-engine)
  2. Location (independent of how the object looks)

My previous outline shader looked at discontinuities in color, depth or normals. This is easy to use, but provides me with little to none artistic control over the position of the outlines. I had to find another solution.

My next idea was to draw the outlines on the texture of the object itself. While this method provides me control over the location of the outlines, it gives me no control over the visuals of the outlines inside of Unity. Once I painted the texture, the lines are there and if I want them to look different, I need to repaint them.
